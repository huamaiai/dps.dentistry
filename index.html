The Transformation

From availability-dependent enquiries → protected demand, using our 3-step activation process.

Step 1: Overflow & Out-of-Hours Activation

A Demand Protection Layer is configured around your phone handling and booking structure, then activates automatically when reception cannot answer.

  • Line busy
  • No answer after a set number of rings
  • Outside opening hours
Step 2: Treatment Call Capture + Practice-Approved Next Step

When the system activates, it answers the call, captures the caller’s details, understands what they are enquiring about, and follows your agreed handling rules.
That may mean booking a suitable consultation, creating a callback task, flagging the call for a TCO, or sending the team a structured summary for follow-up.

  • Captures caller details
  • Identifies treatment interest
  • Separates serious treatment calls from general questions
  • Applies your agreed handling rules
  • Books only where appropriate
  • Sends the team a structured call summary
Step 3: Demand Visibility

You receive visibility into treatment demand that previously disappeared during busy periods and after hours.

  • Enquiries captured outside opening hours
  • Enquiries captured during busy periods
  • Consultations booked through the system

What Demand Protection does not do

Because high-value dentistry should never be handled like a simple transaction.

It does not diagnose

Clinical judgement stays with the practice. The system captures context, not clinical decisions.

It does not quote treatment

It can follow your approved guidance, but it does not turn complex dentistry into price shopping.

It does not replace your team

It activates only when the team cannot answer, then follows the next step according to your process.

Common questions practice owners ask

“Why not just call them back tomorrow?”

Because many implant and aligner enquiries contact multiple practices at once. If another practice answers immediately and offers a slot, that consultation is often booked there — even if you call back later. Demand Protection captures the enquiry at the moment it happens.

“Patients can book or request a callback from our website.”

That helps — and Demand Protection complements it. Many high-value callers still phone to ask a quick question, confirm availability or speak to someone before committing. If the call reaches voicemail, they usually try the next practice rather than filling a form and waiting.

“Existing patients usually are loyal.”

This is for new private treatment callers who have not yet built trust with the practice.
Until that first conversation happens, they are still deciding who to speak to, who feels responsive, and who they trust enough to take the next step with.

“Our reception usually answers.”

Most of the time, yes. This is for the moments they can’t — busy periods and after-hours windows where demand quietly books elsewhere. It only needs to happen occasionally to matter.

“Dentistry isn’t something patients should shop around for.”

Agreed. High-value dentistry is built on trust, clinical judgement and proper assessment.
Demand Protection is not designed to help patients compare complex treatment like a commodity.
It is designed to make sure that when a serious treatment call comes in, the practice captures enough context to start the right conversation instead of losing the call before the relationship begins.
